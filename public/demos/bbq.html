🌿 Since 1987

Three Generations
of Smoke

It started with Grandpa Earl Beaumont and a hand-welded smoker he built in his backyard in Mason, Texas. Earl didn't have a recipe book — he had instinct, patience, and a whole lot of post oak wood.

He'd fire up that old pit before the sun rose, season his briskets by feel, and let the smoke do the talking for fourteen hours straight. Neighbors started showing up. Word spread. By 1992, Smoke & Soul BBQ was a real honest-to-goodness restaurant on Smokehouse Road.

Earl passed the tongs to his son Darnell in 2001, who added the legendary Holy Smoke Ribs to the menu. Now it's Lena Beaumont — Earl's granddaughter — running the pits, keeping the traditions alive, and adding a few innovations of her own.

Some things never change: the wood, the patience, and the soul.
