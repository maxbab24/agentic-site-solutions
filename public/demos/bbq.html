Three Generations. One Pit.

Grandpa Earl fired up his first pit in Mason, Texas in 1987. He smoked through the night, ready by noon. By 2 o’clock the line stretched down the block and around the corner. He sold out in forty minutes.

Earl’s son Roy took over the tongs in 1998, adding the jalapeño sausage that still sells out by 1 p.m. every Friday. Roy’s daughter, Claire, runs the pits now — same post oak, same rub recipe written in pencil on a notecard tacked inside the smokehouse door.

We don’t have a drive-through. We don’t have a timer. What we have is smoke, patience, and thirty-seven years of knowing what good brisket tastes like when nobody rushed it.
